## Abstract

This working paper examines data governance as a foundational infrastructure for the digital transformation and AI readiness of judicial institutions in Africa. It argues that without strong, structured, and institutionally embedded data governance frameworks, digital transformation efforts in the justice sector risk producing fragmented systems, unreliable data, weak institutional learning, and unsafe AI adoption. Drawing on data governance theory, digital government research, and institutional analysis, the paper develops a conceptual framework for Judicial Data Governance and outlines its core dimensions, governance requirements, and institutional conditions.
