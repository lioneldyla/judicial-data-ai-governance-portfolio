## 1. Context

SPJEJ is a composite statistical index designed to measure judicial
performance across African court systems. It is grounded in the
researcher's prior professional practice at the Ministry of Justice
Statistics Unit (Côte d'Ivoire), where the observed gap between
disparate performance indicators and the absence of a synthesizing
index motivated the design.

## 2. Problem

- Judicial statistics in West Africa are typically reported as disparate
  indicators (cases filed, cases resolved, average duration), without a
  composite view of institutional performance.
- Existing international indices (World Justice Project, Doing Business,
  CEPEJ) are calibrated for their own contexts and do not fit
  resource-constrained OHADA-region justice systems.
- Decision-makers need a synthesized measurement to prioritize
  interventions and track progress over time.

## 3. Methodology

Design-science approach:

1. **Requirements elicitation** — expert interviews with judicial
   administrators, statistical officers, and policy actors.
2. **Indicator selection** — literature review + practitioner
   consultation to identify candidate indicators.
3. **Weighting** — analytic hierarchy process (AHP) or principal-
   component analysis (PCA); sensitivity analysis.
4. **Pilot data collection** — Côte d'Ivoire judicial statistics
   yearbook data (2019 & 2023) as the primary source.
5. **Validation** — expert review; internal consistency checks;
   comparison against qualitative institutional assessments.
6. **Publication** — technical note documenting index construction and
   pilot results.

Data sources:

- Judicial Statistics Yearbooks of Côte d'Ivoire (2019, 2023) — public
- Institutional performance reports — access via institutional authorization
