## 3. Research Problem

Justice systems in Africa are undergoing a transformation driven by digitization, data proliferation, and the emergence of artificial intelligence tools. This transformation holds significant promise for improving access to justice, reducing delays, and enabling evidence-based judicial governance.

However, this transformation is occurring in an institutional context characterized by:

- Fragmented or absent data governance frameworks
- Inadequate data quality infrastructure
- Limited AI regulatory frameworks at national and regional levels
- Insufficient institutional capacity for AI evaluation and oversight
- Weak integration between judicial information systems and broader public sector digital infrastructure

The central research problem is: **How can judicial institutions in Africa govern judicial data and artificial intelligence systems in ways that strengthen rather than undermine justice, accountability, and the rule of law?**

## 4. Proposed Thesis Direction

### 4.1 Proposed Title

> **"Judicial Data Governance and Artificial Intelligence in African Justice Systems: Toward a Framework for Accountable, Human-Centered, and AI-Ready Judicial Institutions"**

*This title is indicative and will be refined through doctoral supervision.*

### 4.2 Research Questions

**Primary Research Question:**

> What governance frameworks, institutional conditions, and policy instruments are necessary to enable African justice systems to manage judicial data and deploy artificial intelligence in ways consistent with rule of law principles?

**Secondary Research Questions:**

1. What are the current states of judicial data governance in selected African justice systems, and what explains observed variations?
2. How do existing international and regional AI governance frameworks (EU AI Act, UNESCO AI Ethics Recommendation, African Union Data Policy Framework) apply to judicial AI deployment in African contexts?
3. What does a maturity model for judicial data governance look like, and how can it guide progressive institutional development?
4. What conditions — technical, institutional, legal, and cultural — determine the readiness of an African justice system to adopt AI-assisted judicial tools?
5. How can the Judicial Data Governance Framework (JDGF) be validated through application to a real institutional context (Côte d'Ivoire justice system)?

### 4.3 Disciplinary Position

The thesis will be positioned at the intersection of:

- **Administrative law and public governance** (primary disciplinary anchor)
- **Information science and data governance** (methodological contribution)
- **African studies and development law** (contextual contribution)
- **Science and technology studies / AI governance** (thematic contribution)

## 5. Methodology

### 5.1 Methodological Approach

The research adopts a **comparative, multi-method approach** combining:

1. **Systematic literature review** — Mapping existing frameworks and scholarship on judicial data governance and AI in justice systems (global and African contexts)
2. **Comparative case study** — Comparative analysis of data governance practices in 3–5 African justice systems (including Côte d'Ivoire as primary case)
3. **Expert interviews** — Semi-structured interviews with judicial administrators, data governance specialists, AI researchers, and policymakers
4. **Framework development and validation** — Construction and iterative validation of the JDGF through empirical testing
5. **Policy analysis** — Review of national legislation, regional instruments, and institutional policies on judicial data and AI

### 5.2 Case Selection

Primary case: **Côte d'Ivoire** (deep access, researcher embeddedness, institutional cooperation)  
Comparative cases (provisional): Senegal, Rwanda, South Africa, Morocco

Case selection prioritizes: geographic diversity, legal system variation (civil law, common law, hybrid), digital governance maturity variation, OHADA membership.
