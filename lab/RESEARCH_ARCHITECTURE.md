## 1. Research vision

Judicial systems in Africa are undergoing a transformation driven by
digitization, data proliferation, and the emergence of artificial
intelligence tools. This transformation is occurring in an institutional
context characterized by fragmented data governance, inadequate data
quality infrastructure, limited AI regulatory frameworks, insufficient
capacity for AI evaluation, and weak integration of judicial
information systems with broader public-sector infrastructure.

The programme exists to bridge the gap between technological possibility
and institutional reality — to build the evidence base, frameworks, and
tools that enable justice systems to govern their data responsibly,
adopt AI ethically, and serve citizens effectively.

*Source: [`RESEARCH_MANIFESTO.md`](../RESEARCH_MANIFESTO.md) §I.*

## 3. Research questions

The programme's questions are consolidated in
[`RESEARCH_QUESTIONS.md`](RESEARCH_QUESTIONS.md), organized in four
tracks (Judicial Data Governance, AI in Justice, Judicial Statistics
& Open Data, Digital Transformation Governance).

Central research question of the doctoral trajectory:

> How can judicial institutions in Africa govern judicial data and
> artificial intelligence systems in ways that strengthen rather than
> undermine justice, accountability, and the rule of law?

*Source: [`DOCTORAL_RESEARCH_PLAN.md`](../DOCTORAL_RESEARCH_PLAN.md) §3.*

## 5. Methodological relationships

The programme deploys five methodological orientations, applied
across pillars and outputs:

| Method | Where it applies |
|---|---|
| **Institutional analysis** (neo-institutional theory, public admin, org sociology) | Pillars I–III; frameworks; WP01, WP03 |
| **Comparative law** (OHADA, national legislation, regional instruments) | Pillars I–III; WP02, JA01–JA02 |
| **Mixed methods** (qualitative case + quantitative stats) | Pillars I–II; case studies; datasets |
| **Participatory approaches** (practitioners, registry staff, data professionals) | Pillars I–II; case studies; capacity building |
| **Design science** (iterative framework construction) | JDGF, SPJEJ; toolkits |

*Source: [`RESEARCH_STRATEGY_2026-2035.md`](../RESEARCH_STRATEGY_2026-2035.md) §4.*

Target institutions include universities in France (Paris 1 Panthéon-
Sorbonne, Sciences Po Paris, Université de Montpellier), Belgium
(UCLouvain, ULiège), Canada (Université de Montréal, UQAM), and Africa
(Université Félix Houphouët-Boigny, UCAD Dakar, University of Cape Town),
including possible co-tutelle arrangements.
