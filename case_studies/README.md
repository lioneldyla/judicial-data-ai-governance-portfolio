## Structure of every case study

Each case study directory contains a `README.md` following this template:

1. **Context** — institutional and geographic setting; time frame; key actors.
2. **Problem** — the observed governance / digital / analytical issue.
3. **Methodology** — how the case is / was studied (data sources, methods).
4. **Implementation** — what was done, by whom, when.
5. **Lessons learned** — what the case teaches, generalisable insights.
6. **Research implications** — how the case informs the Lab's frameworks
   and publications.

Optional additional files: `documents/`, `data/`, `figures/`, `interviews/`,
`timeline.md`, `evidence.md`.

---

## Editorial policy

- **No fabricated evidence.** Every claim of fact in a case study is
  supported by a documented source, or clearly labelled as
  `[TO BE DOCUMENTED]`.
- **No unauthorized publication of sensitive data.** Confidential judicial
  information is never published without appropriate authorization from
  the source institution. See
  [`../DATA_GOVERNANCE_PRINCIPLES.md`](../DATA_GOVERNANCE_PRINCIPLES.md).
- **Positionality is disclosed.** Every case study specifies the
  researcher's role in the case (participant / observer / analyst).
- **Version-controlled.** Substantive revisions of a case study are
  logged in the Portfolio's [`../CHANGELOG.md`](../CHANGELOG.md).

## What is NOT here

- Case studies that have not been substantively engaged with. Only the
  five above have concrete connection points in the programme.
- Cases without an author of record. Every case study has a documented
  positionality.

*Additional case studies are added when a specific research need identifies
them and the researcher has (or is preparing to obtain) direct
engagement with the case.*
