# Judicial Data & AI Governance Lab

> **JDAG Lab** — the scientific identity behind the *Data Governance for
> Intelligent Justice Systems* research programme.

---

## Mission

To produce rigorous, independent, and practice-relevant research on the
governance of judicial data systems and artificial intelligence in African
justice institutions, so as to strengthen accountability, improve data
quality, and support the ethical adoption of digital technologies in the
delivery of justice.

*Source of authority: [`LAB_CHARTER.md`](../LAB_CHARTER.md) §2.*

## Vision

A future in which every African justice system:

- governs its data as a strategic public asset;
- adopts artificial intelligence within accountable, rights-respecting frameworks;
- produces reliable, accessible judicial statistics that inform policy and public trust;
- trains its personnel to understand, challenge, and govern digital systems;
- contributes to regional and global knowledge on digital justice governance.

*Source of authority: [`RESEARCH_MANIFESTO.md`](../RESEARCH_MANIFESTO.md) §IV.*

> *From Data to Justice. From Governance to Trust.*

## Research programme

**Data Governance for Intelligent Justice Systems** — a ten-year
programme (2026–2035) articulated around three interdependent pillars.

| Pillar | Theme | Horizon |
|---|---|---|
| **I — Data Governance** | Judicial data governance frameworks, data quality, judicial statistics, open data in justice | 2026–2028 |
| **II — Digital Justice** | Digital transformation of court administration, e-justice, registry modernisation, legal tech | 2026–2029 |
| **III — AI Governance** | Responsible AI in judicial decision-making, algorithmic accountability, AI regulation for African public sectors | 2026–2035 |

*Source of authority: [`RESEARCH_STRATEGY_2026-2035.md`](../RESEARCH_STRATEGY_2026-2035.md), [`README.md`](../README.md).*
