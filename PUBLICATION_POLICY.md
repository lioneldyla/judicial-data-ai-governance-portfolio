## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document governs the production, release, and dissemination of all research outputs associated with the **Data Governance for Intelligent Justice Systems** research programme. It applies to all publication types: working papers, journal articles, conference papers, policy briefs, technical reports, and book contributions.

The policy ensures quality, consistency, attribution accuracy, and alignment with open science commitments.

---

## 2. Publication Types

### 2.1 Working Papers (WP)

Working papers are the primary vehicle for disseminating research in progress. They are citable from the moment of publication and may be submitted to journals after peer feedback.

**Standards:**
- Minimum length: 6,000 words
- Abstract: 200–300 words
- Keywords: 5–10 terms
- License: CC BY 4.0
- Repository path: `working_papers/WPXX/`
- DOI registration: Zenodo (upon first release)
- Identifier format: `WP` + two-digit sequential number (e.g., `WP01`, `WP02`)

**Version scheme for working papers:**

| Stage | Version |
|-------|--------|
| First draft (circulated) | v0.1 |
| Revised after feedback | v0.2 — v0.9 |
| Published preprint | v1.0 |
| Revised after peer review | v1.1+ |
| Published (accepted) | v2.0 |

### 2.2 Journal Articles

Journal articles represent fully peer-reviewed work. Author accepted manuscripts are deposited in Zenodo concurrent with journal publication.

**Standards:**
- Target journals: Q1/Q2 in law, governance, information science, or African studies
- Open access: required (gold OA or green OA via repository)
- Repository path: `journal_articles/`
- Preprint: must be registered on SSRN prior to submission

### 2.3 Conference Papers

Conference papers are full-length submissions to peer-reviewed academic conferences.

**Standards:**
- Minimum length: 4,000 words (full paper); 1,500 words (extended abstract)
- Repository path: `conference_papers/`
- Presentation slides: deposited in `presentations/` with DOI

### 2.4 Policy Briefs

Policy briefs are 2–4 page documents targeting institutional and policy audiences. They are derived from working papers or journal articles and do not constitute independent research.

**Standards:**
- Structure: Problem / Evidence / Recommendation
- Audience: policymakers, institutional decision-makers
- License: CC BY 4.0

### 2.5 Technical Reports

Technical reports document methodology, system architecture, data models, or implementation details associated with research projects.

**Standards:**
- Include version history and changelog
- Archive with source code when applicable
- Repository path: `software/` or `frameworks/`

## 3. Authorship Policy

### 3.1 Authorship Criteria

Authorship follows the **ICMJE Recommendations** (International Committee of Medical Journal Editors), adapted for social science and law:

1. Substantial contribution to conception, design, data collection, or analysis
2. Drafting or critically revising the intellectual content
3. Final approval of the version to be published
4. Accountability for all aspects of the work

### 3.2 AI Assistance Declaration

AI tools (including language models) are used in this programme as writing assistance tools. They do not qualify for authorship. All uses of AI assistance in the drafting process are declared in the Author Contributions or Acknowledgements section.

### 3.3 Acknowledgements

All substantive assistance — from collaborators, institutions, data providers, and funding bodies — must be acknowledged.

---

## 4. Peer Review Process

### 4.1 Internal Review

All working papers undergo an internal quality review before release:

1. Author completes draft (v0.x)
2. Self-review against quality checklist (structure, citations, Markdown rendering)
3. Release for community feedback (v1.0)

### 4.2 External Review

Working papers submitted to journals undergo standard double-blind peer review. The repository version is updated to reflect revisions; the version history is documented in the document header.

---

## 5. Citation and Reference Standards

### 5.1 Citation Style

- Primary style: **APA 7th Edition** for all publications
- Alternative: Chicago 17th (for legal scholarship)
- In-text: Author (Year) or (Author, Year)
- Bibliography: Full reference list at end of document

### 5.2 Self-Citation

Self-citation of prior work in this programme is encouraged for coherence and cumulative knowledge building, provided the cited work is accessible (open access or repository deposit).

### 5.3 Citation of Repository Outputs

All repository outputs include citation instructions in the document header. The master citation instruction is in `CITATION.cff`.
