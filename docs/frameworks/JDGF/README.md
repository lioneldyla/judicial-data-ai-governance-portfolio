## Overview

The **Judicial Data Governance Framework (JDGF)** is an evidence-based, Africa-centred framework designed to enable national judiciaries to govern judicial data responsibly, ensure data quality, and support institutional accountability. The JDGF addresses the specific legal, institutional, and infrastructural realities of African courts while aligning with international standards (GDPR principles, OECD data governance recommendations, AU Data Policy Framework).

This framework is the primary research output of Pillar I of the *Data Governance for Intelligent Justice Systems* research programme.

---

## Framework Objectives

1. **Establish** minimum standards for judicial data collection, storage, and dissemination
2. **Operationalise** data quality dimensions within court administration contexts
3. **Enable** interoperability between judicial information systems at national and regional levels
4. **Protect** sensitive judicial data while promoting transparency and open justice
5. **Provide** a practical implementation pathway for resource-constrained judiciaries

---

## Framework Components

### Component 1 — Governance Architecture
Institutional arrangements, roles, and responsibilities for judicial data governance. Includes data stewardship models adapted for civil-law court systems.

### Component 2 — Data Quality Standards
Operational definitions and measurement approaches for the six judicial data quality dimensions: accuracy, completeness, timeliness, consistency, accessibility, and traceability.

### Component 3 — Legal and Regulatory Alignment
Mapping of JDGF requirements against applicable national laws, OHADA instruments, AU data governance frameworks, and international human rights standards.

### Component 4 — Implementation Methodology
Phased implementation roadmap, diagnostic tools, and capacity-building requirements for judicial institutions adopting the framework.

### Component 5 — Monitoring and Evaluation
Key performance indicators, audit protocols, and reporting mechanisms for sustained governance quality.
