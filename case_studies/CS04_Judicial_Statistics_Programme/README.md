## 1. Context

The Judicial Statistics Programme of Côte d'Ivoire produces national
judicial statistics yearbooks that consolidate court activity data from
across the national court system. Two yearbooks are in scope for this
case study: **2019** and **2023**.

The `judicial-statistics` repository (public, on the researcher's
GitHub) hosts the data pipelines and methodology supporting the
yearbooks.

The researcher's professional role (2018–2021) provides direct
knowledge of the 2019 yearbook production cycle, with the 2023 cycle
observed at greater distance.

## 2. Problem

- Yearbook production is time-consuming and depends on court-level data
  collection under variable conditions.
- Documentation of the methodology has historically lived in internal
  documents rather than public references.
- Reproducibility and comparability across yearbook cycles is a
  recurring practitioner concern.

## 3. Methodology

Documentation + comparative-analysis case:

1. **Documentary analysis** — reconstruct the 2019 and 2023 methodologies
   from institutional documents and public yearbook publications.
2. **Process analysis** — map data flows from courts to the national
   consolidation.
3. **Data quality analysis** — examine quality signals (completeness,
   timeliness, consistency) using pipeline outputs.
4. **Comparative analysis** — identify continuities and changes between
   the two cycles.
5. **Publication** — case study writing + WP03 contribution + technical
   note.

## 6. Research implications

- **SPJEJ (CS01, P03):** yearbook data is the primary SPJEJ pilot input.
- **JDGF (P02):** the yearbook process illustrates the JDGF's *Data*,
  *Processes*, and *Performance* domains applied at national scale.
- **WP01:** empirical grounding for §3 (Judicial Data as a Category)
  and §5 (Institutional Conditions).
- **WP03:** the yearbook cycle is a substantive empirical anchor for
  the JDGF full articulation.
