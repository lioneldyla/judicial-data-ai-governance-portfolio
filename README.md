## 🌍 Research Vision

This repository is the **official Research Digital Infrastructure (RDI)** of **DJORO Yoboukoua Lionel Arnaud**, supporting the complete scientific lifecycle of the **Data Governance for Intelligent Justice Systems** research programme.

The overarching vision is to design, validate, and disseminate **evidence-based frameworks** enabling African judiciaries to govern data responsibly, integrate AI ethically, and achieve measurable improvements in access to justice, transparency, and institutional integrity — consistent with the rule of law and African continental integration objectives (AU Agenda 2063, ECOWAS Digital Agenda).

This infrastructure adheres to the **FAIR principles** (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) and is designed for long-term integration with ORCID, Zenodo, SSRN, OpenAIRE, and GitHub Pages.

> *From Data to Justice. From Governance to Trust.*

## 📐 Research Programme

The research programme **Data Governance for Intelligent Justice Systems** articulates around three interdependent pillars:

| Pillar | Theme | Horizon |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **I — Data Governance** | Judicial data governance frameworks, data quality, judicial statistics, open data in justice | 2026–2028 |
| **II — Digital Justice** | Digital transformation of court administration, e-justice, registry modernisation, legal tech | 2026–2029 |
| **III — AI Governance** | Responsible AI in judicial decision-making, algorithmic accountability, AI regulation for African public sectors | 2026–2035 |

## 🏗️ Research Frameworks

### JDGF — Judicial Data Governance Framework

The **JDGF** is the flagship methodological framework of the programme. It provides a comprehensive governance model for designing, implementing, evaluating, and continuously improving judicial data ecosystems in support of trustworthy, accountable, human-centered, and AI-ready justice systems.

→ [Full framework documentation](frameworks/JDGF/)

### SPJEJ — Statistical Performance Index for Justice Efficiency

A composite index designed to measure judicial statistical performance across African court systems.

→ Planned — 2026 Q4

### CS GREFFE OS — Open Source Court Registry System

An open-source digital registry platform for African courts, designed for low-resource deployment.

→ Planned — 2027
