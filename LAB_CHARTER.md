## 2. Mission

To produce rigorous, independent, and practice-relevant research on the governance of judicial data systems and artificial intelligence in African justice institutions, with the objective of strengthening accountability, improving data quality, and supporting the ethical adoption of digital technologies in the delivery of justice.

---

## 3. Scientific Governance

### 3.1 Research Independence

All research is conducted with full scientific independence. No institutional affiliation, employment relationship, or funding source confers authority over research findings, methodology, or publication decisions.

### 3.2 Quality Standards

All outputs shall meet the following minimum standards:

- Written in clear, precise academic English or French
- Supported by documented sources and transparent methodology
- Reviewed against relevant literature before publication
- Versioned and archived with persistent identifiers

### 3.3 Open Science Obligations

All research outputs shall be:

- Deposited in Zenodo with a persistent DOI
- Published under an open license (CC BY 4.0 for written works; MIT or Apache 2.0 for software)
- Referenced in ORCID and OpenAIRE profiles
- Cited in the repository's CITATION.cff

## 4. Document Governance

### 4.1 Version Control

All documents shall follow Semantic Versioning:
- **Major versions** (1.0, 2.0): substantive revisions changing content or structure
- **Minor versions** (1.1, 1.2): additions or meaningful improvements
- **Patch versions** (1.0.1, 1.0.2): editorial corrections

All repository files shall follow the naming conventions defined in the [Contributing Guidelines](./CONTRIBUTING.md).
