## 1. Context

Court digital transformation is a live agenda in Côte d'Ivoire and
across the OHADA region. National strategies, donor-funded projects,
and institutional initiatives together shape a complex, uneven
transformation trajectory.

This case study is the *comparative* case for the Digital Transformation
Governance research track. The primary case is Côte d'Ivoire; comparative
sketches include Senegal, Rwanda, South Africa, and Morocco.

## 2. Problem

- Digital transformation in justice is driven simultaneously by
  institutional actors, donors, and technology suppliers — governance
  responsibility is distributed and sometimes unclear.
- Transformation initiatives that succeed technically may fail
  institutionally (adoption, capacity, sustainability).
- The governance question is: what institutional configurations enable
  successful, sustainable, rights-respecting digital transformation of
  courts?

## 3. Methodology

Multi-country comparative institutional analysis:

1. **Country-level documentary analysis** — national judicial
   digitalisation strategies, institutional mandates, donor-funded
   project documents.
2. **Comparative sketches** — Senegal, Rwanda, South Africa, Morocco.
3. **Governance mapping** — actors, decision rights, funding flows,
   accountability arrangements.
4. **Outcome analysis** — what changed institutionally after
   digitalisation efforts?
5. **Framework synthesis** — patterns generalisable to WP04.
