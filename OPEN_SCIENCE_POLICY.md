## 1. Statement of Commitment

The **Data Governance for Intelligent Justice Systems** research programme is committed to the principles and practices of Open Science. This commitment is not merely procedural — it reflects a fundamental conviction that research on justice systems, data governance, and artificial intelligence must itself be transparent, reproducible, and accessible if it is to serve the public interest it studies.

Open Science in the context of this programme means:

- Publishing research outputs under open licenses wherever legally and ethically possible
- Making data Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable (FAIR)
- Sharing research software as open-source
- Registering preprints before or alongside peer review
- Documenting research methodology with sufficient detail for independent reproduction
- Engaging openly with researchers, practitioners, and communities

## 2. FAIR Data Principles

All datasets produced or used in this programme shall adhere to the FAIR data principles as defined by Wilkinson et al. (2016) and endorsed by the European Commission, African Open Science Platform, and international research funders.

### 2.1 Findable

- Datasets are assigned persistent identifiers (DOI via Zenodo)
- Datasets are described with rich metadata in standard schemas (Dublin Core, DataCite, Schema.org)
- Metadata is registered in searchable repositories

### 2.2 Accessible

- Datasets are deposited in open repositories (Zenodo primary; institutional repositories secondary)
- Authentication and authorization requirements are documented explicitly
- Where full openness is not possible (e.g., personal judicial data), access protocols are published

### 2.3 Interoperable

- Data is shared in open, non-proprietary formats (CSV, JSON, RDF, OWL)
- Controlled vocabularies and ontologies are used where applicable
- Data models are documented and versioned

### 2.4 Reusable

- All datasets include a usage license (CC BY 4.0 preferred; CC BY-SA 4.0 for copyleft)
- Provenance and collection methodology are documented
- Data quality indicators are declared

## 3. Publication Openness

### 3.1 Open Access

All working papers, technical reports, and policy briefs are published open access by default. Journal submissions target venues with open access options. Author accepted manuscripts are deposited in Zenodo and SSRN no later than the journal embargo deadline.

### 3.2 Preprints

Preprints are registered on SSRN and/or arXiv before or concurrent with peer-reviewed submission. Preprints carry the DOI of the repository deposit and link to the final publication upon acceptance.

### 3.3 Reproducibility

All quantitative analyses include the code, data, and environment specifications required for independent replication. Qualitative research includes detailed methodology appendices.

---

## 4. Research Software

### 4.1 Open Source

Research software and analysis tools developed in this programme are released as open source. The primary license is **MIT License** for tools and libraries, and **GPL-3.0** for frameworks intended to remain open.

### 4.2 Software Citation

Software is deposited on Zenodo and assigned a DOI. CITATION.cff files are maintained for all significant software outputs. Users are requested to cite the software when using it in their own work.

### 4.3 Versioning

Software follows **Semantic Versioning 2.0.0** (MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH). Releases are tagged in Git and documented in CHANGELOG.md.

## 6. Ethical Constraints on Openness

Open Science commitments operate within ethical and legal boundaries:

1. **Judicial data confidentiality.** Datasets containing individual judicial records, case data, or personal information of parties are not published in open repositories. Anonymized or aggregate versions may be published where scientifically valid.
2. **National security and sovereignty.** Data that could compromise judicial institution security is excluded from open publication.
3. **Indigenous and community data.** Research involving community-held knowledge follows the CARE Principles (Collective Benefit, Authority to Control, Responsibility, Ethics) alongside FAIR.
